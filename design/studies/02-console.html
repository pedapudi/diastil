Study 02 — the console · dense observatory

The bet. The document's structure is the product's honesty made visible. The tree shows what ingest actually understood — roles, scene node/edge counts, per-slide fidelity scores, islands flagged in caution — and the op log shows every mutation, yours and the copilot's, in one auditable stream. Nothing the editor believes is hidden.
What it optimizes. Trust after ingest (the 0.91 slide and the island are one glance away); precision work on complex decks; a power user's sense of place (crumbs: deck › slide › figure). The copilot is a full-height peer, and its accepted ops appear in the same log as manual ones — provenance for free.
What it costs. Chrome-heavy: the canvas gets ~55% of the width at 1440px. Properties have no fixed home — they'd ride in a hovercard/popover near the selection, which is elegant but less discoverable than a docked inspector. Reads as an instrument, which may intimidate the casual "just fix my title" user.
