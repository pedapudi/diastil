Study 04 — the light table · continuous flow

The bet. A deck is a sequence, and most editing sins are sequence sins — rhythm breaks, inconsistent scale, a diagram that contradicts its neighbor. Slides flow vertically at reading size, edited in place; the margin gutter carries each slide's metadata (number, fidelity, island flags, notes) like a proof sheet. Grid mode zooms out to the sorting view.
What it optimizes. Typesetting across the whole deck — you see three slides at once, so token edits are judged in context. Ingest honesty rides in the margin, per slide, without a tree. The copilot naturally gets flow context ("sees slides 3–5") — cross-slide asks are its best use.
What it costs. Never more than ~1.5 slides tall on a laptop — fine-grained diagram work wants a zoomed single-slide mode (click a figure → it takes the viewport, stage-style). Present-order dragging happens in grid mode, not here. In-place editing across many mounted slides needs careful virtualization to stay 60fps.
