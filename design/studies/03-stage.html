Study 03 — the stage · canvas-first, transient chrome

The bet. The slide is the interface. All chrome is transient: tools appear at the selection (the floating toolbar is shape/anchor/ route/align for a node, scale-step/face/tracking for text), the filmstrip floats and auto-hides, the copilot lives in a strip until summoned (⌘.), and ⌘K is an op palette for everything else. Mid-drag state is the hero: handles, anchors, live dashed reroutes, red smart guides with the gap math.
What it optimizes. Diagramming — this is the tldraw/Figma-grade loop the plan promises, with maximum canvas at every screen size. Also the most beautiful demo: the product looks like the decks it makes.
What it costs. Discoverability. Write-target visibility — the bench's "this edit hits the token" line — has to squeeze into the floating toolbar or a toast, and it matters too much to bury. Structure, fidelity, and islands are invisible unless summoned. Highest interaction-polish tax: floating chrome must never occlude what you're editing.
