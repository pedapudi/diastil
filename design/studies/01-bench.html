Study 01 — the bench · classic three-pane

The bet. Familiarity. Filmstrip left, canvas center, inspector right is the grammar every deck editor since 1997 has taught; nothing needs explaining. The inspector leads with the write-target line — the one diastil-specific idea here: you always see whether an edit lands on a token, a rule, or this element, before it lands.
What it optimizes. Typesetting flow: persistent type controls, token bindings visible as chips, copilot always docked but subordinate (fixed height, below the inspector). Deck-wide edits feel safe because the target is declared.
What it costs. The right rail is contested — inspector and copilot split it, both cramped. Diagram tools have nowhere natural to live (they'd swap into the inspector on selection). Structure (roles, islands, ops) is invisible; you trust the canvas. Least distinctive of the four.
